There are neighborhoods that people move to because of the houses, and then there are neighborhoods people stay in because of the lifestyle. Ballast Point is one of those places.

Located along the western shoreline of Hillsborough Bay, just north of MacDill Air Force Base, Ballast Point blends waterfront beauty, rich history, and a true sense of community that has made it one of South Tampa's most beloved neighborhoods.

Every Fourth of July, that pride becomes even more visible. American flags line the streets, neighbors gather with family and friends, and Ballast Point Park becomes one of the best places in Tampa to enjoy the waterfront while watching fireworks light up the night sky. It's the kind of neighborhood where traditions continue year after year and memories are made across generations.

A Neighborhood with a Story Unlike Any Other

One of Ballast Point's most fascinating pieces of history isn't just found along the waterfront—it's connected to the moon. Before humanity had ever launched a rocket into space, French author Jules Verne imagined it.

In his 1865 novel From the Earth to the Moon, Verne chose Tampa as the fictional launch site for mankind's journey to the moon. Decades later, the waterfront park that residents enjoy today was known as Jules Verne Park, honoring that incredible connection between imagination and exploration.

That history inspired the song you'll find on this page. The hook—"From the Earth to the Moon… to Ballast Point"—celebrates both Ballast Point's unique place in literary history and the neighborhood's enduring spirit.

Waterfront Living at Its Best

Ballast Point Park remains one of Tampa's hidden gems. Whether you're fishing from the pier, walking beneath the shaded oak trees, launching a kayak, taking the kids to the playground, or simply enjoying panoramic views of downtown Tampa across Hillsborough Bay, it's easy to understand why so many people fall in love with this community.

For runners, walkers, and cyclists, nearby Bayshore Boulevard offers one of the most scenic waterfront routes anywhere in Florida.

A Blend of Old Florida and Modern Living

One of Ballast Point's greatest strengths is its character. Historic homes with large front porches sit alongside beautifully renovated residences and thoughtfully designed new construction. Mature oak trees, quiet streets, and established landscaping give the neighborhood a timeless feel that's becoming increasingly rare throughout Tampa Bay.

It's a place where neighbors know each other, children ride bikes through the neighborhood, and evenings are often spent on front porches or along the waterfront.

Local Favorites

Part of Ballast Point's charm comes from its local businesses. Whether you're enjoying dinner at Bocelli Modern Italian, grabbing Mediterranean flavors at Bayshore Mediterranean Grill, picking up a lobster roll from Leon's Lobstah Shack, or ordering a classic slice from Reziero's NY Pizza, the neighborhood offers plenty of great places to gather with family and friends. The nearby Tampa Yacht Club also adds to the area's waterfront identity and long-standing traditions.

More Than Real Estate

As a Realtor who specializes in South Tampa, I believe neighborhoods deserve to be understood—not just measured by square footage or home prices. Every neighborhood has a personality. Every street has stories. Every park creates memories.

Ballast Point is one of those rare communities where history, waterfront living, military pride, local businesses, and neighborhood traditions all come together to create something special. That's why I created the original song featured on this page. It's my way of celebrating not only the Fourth of July but also one of South Tampa's most iconic neighborhoods.

So whether you're a lifelong resident, someone thinking about moving here, or simply discovering Ballast Point for the first time, I hope the music gives you a small glimpse into what makes this community so unique.

Turn the volume up. Wave the American flag. And celebrate everything that makes Ballast Point feel like home. Happy Fourth of July! 🇺🇸
