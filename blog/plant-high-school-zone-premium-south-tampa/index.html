Plant High School Zone Premium in South Tampa

By Ignacio Toraño · July 15, 2026 · Agile Group Realty

The Plant High School attendance zone isn't just a boundary on a map—it's a financial line that adds tens of thousands of dollars to a home's value.

Most buyers I work with assume South Tampa is one unified market. It's not. Plant High School's attendance zone commands a measurable premium, and I'm talking about homes that routinely sell 12 to 18 percent above comparable properties just outside the boundary. I've seen this hold true on streets like Lois Avenue and throughout Palma Ceia, even as interest rates have climbed. That premium reflects how much families value this particular school assignment.

Here's where it gets tricky: those zone boundaries don't follow intuitive lines. You can buy on one side of a street and land in Plant's zone, while the house directly across falls into a completely different assignment. I've watched this play out near the Westshore corridor and along the edges of Sunset Park. A buyer who assumes a listing falls inside Plant's zone based on the neighborhood name or even the listing description can end up very disappointed. Before you make an offer, cross-reference the Hillsborough County Schools boundary lookup tool with the specific parcel address. Not the street. Not the general area. The exact address.

School zones also shift periodically. What was accurate two years ago may not reflect the current assignments, and those changes can alter property values overnight. I've seen buyers overpay based on an outdated assumption, and I've watched others walk away from genuine value because they didn't verify the facts. If you're serious about buying in South Tampa, this is not a detail you skip. It's one of the most consequential decisions in the entire process.

If you're navigating school zones or trying to understand where the real value sits in South Tampa, let's talk. I work this market every day, and I can help you avoid costly assumptions. Whether you're focused on Plant's zone or exploring other parts of the area, I'll make sure you have accurate information before you move forward.
