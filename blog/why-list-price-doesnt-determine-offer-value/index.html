Why List Price Doesn't Determine Offer Value

By Ignacio Toraño · June 2026 · Agile Group Realty

Too many buyers think making an offer is about splitting the difference between what the seller is asking and what they want to pay—but that's not how market value works.

The right offer price starts with price per square foot of closed comps within the last 90 days, adjusted for condition, lot size, and days on market. In South Tampa, a home on Bayshore Boulevard prices differently than a comparable square footage on Estrella Street, even within the same zip code. Hyperlocal data matters more than neighborhood averages, and that's something I drill down on with every buyer I work with.

The issue is that buyers anchoring to list price instead of market data either overpay in a low-inventory pocket or lose a well-priced home by coming in too cautious. I've seen both happen more times than I can count. Active listings are asking prices—they tell you what a seller hopes to get. Closed sales are what the market actually accepted, and that's where we need to start. If you want to get serious about making a competitive offer, we have to look at what similar homes in Palma Ceia or New Suburb Beautiful actually closed for, not what they listed at.

Factor in how long the home sat, any price reductions, and the micro-conditions of the street or block. A house that sits for 45 days with two price cuts tells a different story than one that goes under contract in a week at full ask. When I'm building an offer strategy, I'm not guessing—I'm pulling the data that matters. You can read more about how South Tampa's market moves and why understanding your specific pocket is everything. I've seen the gap between list price and true market value surprise buyers on both ends more often than they expect. The key is knowing what the data actually says before you put pen to paper.
