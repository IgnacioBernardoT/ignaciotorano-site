How Long Does It Take to Close on a Home in Florida

By Ignacio Toraño · June 2026 · Agile Group Realty

Closing on a home feels like it should be simple, but Florida has its own rhythm, and it rarely moves the way buyers expect.

In Florida, most residential closings take 30 to 45 days from contract to keys. Cash deals can close in as little as 10 to 14 days if everything lines up perfectly. Financed purchases depend heavily on the lender, the loan type, and how clean the title comes back. In South Tampa neighborhoods like Palma Ceia or Bayshore Beautiful, title searches can surface old easements or HOA paperwork that adds days to the process without warning.

The issue I see most often is buyers underestimating how much the inspection period, appraisal timeline, and lender review stack on top of each other. One delayed document from a condo association on Swann Avenue can push a closing back a week. It is not always about what goes wrong, but about how much has to go right at the same time. I have watched smooth contracts turn into stressful closings simply because nobody flagged the timeline risk early enough.

The solution is building a realistic timeline from day one and staying in close contact with your title company. Florida requires a licensed title company or attorney to handle closing, so choosing a responsive one matters more than most buyers realize. If you are working with a lender, ask them upfront what their current turnaround time is for underwriting and final approval. That alone can save you from surprises later.
