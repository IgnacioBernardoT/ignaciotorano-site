Buying Historic Homes in South Tampa

By Ignacio Toraño · July 2026 · Agile Group Realty

Historic homes in South Tampa are beautiful, but they are not just older versions of new construction—they are a completely different purchase.

Hyde Park, Courier City, and Palma Ceia are full of charming bungalows and Craftsman homes that look move-in ready, but behind those lovely facades are layers of history that require a very specific kind of due diligence. Even in well-maintained properties, you will often find older plumbing, knob and tube wiring, and original single-pane windows. These things are not dealbreakers, but they do require awareness going in.

The issue most buyers miss is not the home itself—it is the insurance. Historic homes in South Tampa can be difficult and expensive to insure, especially when original materials like plaster walls or old-growth hardwood floors need to be matched or restored after a claim. Standard carriers often will not write the policy at all. I have watched buyers fall in love with a home on Platt Street or Morrison Avenue and then get completely blindsided by the insurance piece two weeks before closing.

The solution is simple but often overlooked: work with an insurance broker who specifically understands historic properties before you close, not after. Getting that conversation started early can prevent a deal from falling apart at the finish line. If you are exploring South Tampa neighborhoods and thinking about a historic home, let me know—I can connect you with the right people and walk you through what to expect long before you make an offer.

Buying a historic home in South Tampa is about appreciating what makes them special while going in with your eyes wide open. The charm is real, but so is the complexity. With the right team in place, it can be one of the most rewarding decisions you make.
