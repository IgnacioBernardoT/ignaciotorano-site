5 Things to Know Before Buying in South Tampa

May 2026 · By Ignacio Toraño

South Tampa is one of the most sought-after areas in all of Florida — and as someone born and raised here, I can tell you the hype is real. But the market moves fast, and there are things every buyer needs to know before jumping in.

1. Understand What "South Tampa" Really Means

When locals say "South Tampa," they're generally referring to everything south of Kennedy Boulevard and west of the Hillsborough River. This includes neighborhoods like Hyde Park, Palma Ceia, Davis Islands, Bayshore Beautiful, Beach Park, and parts of Westshore. Each has its own character, price range, and lifestyle — so knowing which sub-neighborhood fits your goals is the first step.

2. Flood Zones Matter

Tampa Bay is surrounded by water, and many South Tampa properties sit in flood zones. This doesn't mean you shouldn't buy — it means you need to understand the flood zone designation (AE, VE, X, etc.), what it means for your insurance costs, and whether the home has been elevated or retrofitted. I always help my clients get this information early so there are no surprises at closing.

3. The Market Moves Fast

Well-priced homes in desirable South Tampa neighborhoods can go under contract within days — sometimes hours. If you're serious about buying, get pre-approved before you start looking, and be ready to move when the right home comes along. Having an agent who knows the market and can act quickly makes a real difference here.

4. Schools Are a Major Factor

South Tampa is home to some of Hillsborough County's top-rated public schools, including:

  • Plant High School — one of the most sought-after high schools in the Tampa Bay area
  • Coleman Middle School — consistently strong academics and community involvement
  • Mitchell, Roosevelt, and Gorrie Elementary — each with devoted parent communities

Even if you don't have kids, school district quality directly impacts property values and resale potential. It's always worth paying attention to.

5. Work with Someone Who Knows the Area

South Tampa isn't just a market — it's a community. The difference between a great buy and a costly mistake often comes down to local knowledge: knowing which streets flood, which blocks are zoned for which schools, where development is happening, and what a fair price really looks like. That's where working with a local expert makes all the difference.

Hyde Park vs. Davis Islands: Which Neighborhood Is Right for You?

April 2026 · By Ignacio Toraño

Hyde Park and Davis Islands are two of South Tampa's crown jewels — but they offer very different living experiences. As someone who grew up in this area and helps clients navigate these neighborhoods every day, here's my honest take on what makes each one special.

Hyde Park: Walkability & Village Charm

Hyde Park is South Tampa's most walkable neighborhood. With Hyde Park Village at its center — featuring boutique shopping, restaurants, and a weekly farmers market — it offers a lifestyle that's hard to replicate anywhere else in Tampa.

  • Best for: Young professionals, couples, and anyone who values walkability and dining
  • Home styles: Historic bungalows, renovated craftsman homes, and modern new construction
  • Price range: Entry-level homes start around $600K, with luxury properties well over $2M
  • Vibe: Urban-meets-historic, active social scene, tree-lined streets

Davis Islands: Waterfront Living & Community

Davis Islands feels like its own little world — a pair of man-made islands just minutes from downtown Tampa. It's surrounded by water, has its own small airport, a yacht club, and a tight-knit community feel that residents love.

  • Best for: Families, boaters, and anyone who wants a quieter pace with waterfront access
  • Home styles: Mediterranean revival, mid-century ranch, and waterfront estates
  • Price range: Starting around $550K, with waterfront homes ranging from $1.5M to $5M+
  • Vibe: Island community, family-oriented, recreational, dog-friendly

So Which One Is Right for You?

If you want walkable nightlife, trendy restaurants, and a vibrant social scene — Hyde Park is your spot. If you want waterfront living, a family-friendly atmosphere, and a true community feel — Davis Islands is hard to beat.

The good news? They're only about 10 minutes apart. And I know both neighborhoods inside and out.

First-Time Home Buyer's Guide to Tampa Bay

March 2026 · By Ignacio Toraño

Buying your first home is one of the biggest decisions you'll ever make — and it should be exciting, not stressful. Here's my straightforward guide to navigating the Tampa Bay real estate market as a first-time buyer.

Step 1: Get Pre-Approved

Before you start browsing listings, talk to a lender and get pre-approved for a mortgage. This tells you exactly what you can afford and shows sellers you're a serious buyer. In a competitive market like Tampa Bay, offers without pre-approval letters rarely get accepted.

Step 2: Define Your Priorities

Tampa Bay is a big area with a lot of variety. Before we start looking, I always ask my clients to think about:

  • Commute: Where do you work? How far are you willing to drive?
  • Lifestyle: Do you want walkability, waterfront, suburban quiet, or urban energy?
  • Schools: Even if you don't have kids yet, school zones affect home values
  • Must-haves vs. nice-to-haves: Garage, yard, pool, updated kitchen — what's non-negotiable?

Step 3: Understand the Local Market

Tampa Bay's market has its own rhythm. Inventory, pricing, and competition vary by neighborhood and season. Working with a local agent who tracks these trends daily — not just someone who pulls up Zillow listings — gives you a real edge.

Step 4: Make a Strong Offer

When you find the right home, your offer needs to stand out. That means competitive pricing, clean terms, and a pre-approval letter ready to go. I help my clients craft offers that get accepted — not just submitted.

Step 5: Navigate Inspections & Closing

Once your offer is accepted, there's still work to do: home inspection, appraisal, insurance, title search, and closing. I walk my clients through every step so nothing falls through the cracks.

How to Sell Your Tampa Home for Top Dollar

February 2026 · By Ignacio Toraño

Selling your home is a big deal — and in a market like Tampa Bay, the right strategy can mean tens of thousands of dollars more in your pocket. Here's what I've learned works best for my sellers.

Price It Right from Day One

Overpricing is the number one mistake sellers make. A home that sits on the market too long loses momentum and buyer interest. I use a combination of recent comparable sales, current market conditions, and deep local knowledge to price homes strategically — generating maximum interest and competitive offers.

First Impressions Matter

Buyers make decisions in the first 30 seconds. That means your home needs to look its best before a single showing:

  • Declutter and depersonalize — let buyers imagine themselves living there
  • Deep clean everything — especially kitchens and bathrooms
  • Boost curb appeal — fresh landscaping, a clean driveway, a welcoming entrance
  • Consider staging — professionally staged homes consistently sell faster and for more

Marketing That Gets Seen

A yard sign and MLS listing aren't enough. My marketing approach includes professional photography, video walkthroughs, social media promotion, and targeted digital advertising. Your home deserves to be seen by as many qualified buyers as possible.

Negotiation & Closing

Getting an offer is just the beginning. I negotiate on my clients' behalf to protect their interests — from price and closing costs to inspection repairs and timelines. My goal is always to get you the best possible outcome with the least amount of stress.

Why South Tampa Remains One of Florida's Most Desirable Areas

January 2026 · By Ignacio Toraño

I'm biased — I was born and raised in South Tampa, and I've chosen to build my life and career here. But the numbers and the demand speak for themselves. Here's why people keep choosing South Tampa.

The Lifestyle

South Tampa offers something rare: a genuine neighborhood feel with big-city amenities. You can jog along Bayshore Boulevard (the world's longest continuous sidewalk), grab dinner at a locally-owned restaurant on South Howard, and be at the beach in 30 minutes. It's the best of Florida living without the tourist crowds.

The Schools

Families consistently choose South Tampa for its schools. Plant High School, in particular, draws families from across the region. The public school options from elementary through high school are strong, and there are excellent private school options as well.

The Community

South Tampa has a tight-knit feel that surprises people. Neighbors know each other. Local businesses thrive. Community events — from Gasparilla to the Hyde Park Art Fair to neighborhood block parties — bring people together year-round. It's the kind of place where you put down roots.

The Investment

South Tampa real estate has historically held and appreciated in value, even during market downturns. Limited land supply, strong demand, excellent schools, and an unbeatable lifestyle make it one of the most resilient real estate markets in Florida.

The Location

Downtown Tampa, Tampa International Airport, the beaches of St. Pete and Clearwater, and all of Tampa Bay's major employers are easily accessible from South Tampa. Whether you work downtown, in Westshore, or remotely — you're in the perfect spot.
